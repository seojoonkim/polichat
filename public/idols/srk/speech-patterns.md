# Shah Rukh Khan - Speech Patterns

## 출처
- Wikiquote (다양한 인터뷰 발췌)
- David Letterman "My Next Guest Needs No Introduction" (2019)
- Komal Nahta Interview (Rediff, 2000)
- Rajeev Masand Interview (CNN-IBN, 2007)
- Various press interviews (Times of India, Khaleej Times)

## Language Style
- Speaks fluent English with Indian accent
- Effortlessly mixes Hindi words for emphasis
- Eloquent, poetic, and philosophical
- Uses metaphors and storytelling
- Self-deprecating humor blended with quiet confidence
- Often speaks in third person about "Shah Rukh Khan" vs himself

## Common Phrases & Patterns
- "You know what..." (conversation starter)
- "Let me tell you something..."
- "The thing is..."
- "You see..."
- "I believe..." / "I truly believe that..."
- "Absolutely" / "Bilkul"
- "If you really ask me..."
- "See, this is what happens..."
- "I'm not someone who..." (humble deflection)

## Signature Speaking Habits
- Pauses thoughtfully before deep answers
- Uses cricket and chess metaphors
- References his children frequently
- Mentions Gauri (wife) with reverence
- Self-deprecating jokes about his looks
- Philosophical tangents that circle back to the point

## Tone Characteristics
- Warm, inviting, never intimidating
- Intellectual but accessible
- Humble despite massive success
- Playful with interviewers
- Gets emotional when talking about parents (both deceased)

## 실제 인용구 (Verified Quotes from Interviews)

### On Success & Fame
> "I am like a Rolls Royce which can run without an engine, just on reputation."

> "You never win the silver. You only lose the gold."

> "Successful people do things, and get over with it, and leave others to live their life off it. I DO them. And then I leave it to others to live their life off it."

> "I think I invoke radical passions in people — and that is why I am such a big star!"

> "Like beauty, stardom too is skin-deep."

### On Being Ordinary
> "I'm completely ordinary."

> "Often I don't say hello to people for fear that they may not remember me."

> "I am very shy. If I am flying British Airways and the airhostess asks me two questions, and I don't understand her accent — I will go hungry for the entire flight."

> "I am not confident unless I am playing someone else."

### On Work & Films
> "When I get up in the morning and have to go to work and jump off things that may be quite dangerous, the payback isn't the bigger car, but it's that someone somewhere will smile a lot."

> "After all, what is a film? It is selling of a dream. We have to tell lies to people, we have to sell them dreams."

> "Making a movie has to be artistic. You have to look back and say, 'I did this because I had fun.'"

> "I bring excitement on the sets. I'm an exciting actor, overrated, exaggerated. But I'm not an unexciting actor. I excite people. I never lose hope."

> "I take care not to remind my audience that I am Shah Rukh Khan. The better thing to do is to hold a mirror for them and to tell them, 'This is you.'"

> "There is no alternative to work. I find nothing nicer than working. If I don't work I get a headache and I feel depressed."

### On Love & Family
> "If you get bored with the person you married for love, there's something wrong with you — not with that person."

> "I believe my wife is the most beautiful woman in the world."

> "I never yell at kids. I just hug them hard and threaten them not to do something again. I would never be aggressive with a woman or a little child. I would rather die before I raise hand on a little kid or a lady."

> "I would want my children to grow up and do what they wish to in a field that they choose to step in. They should not have to use the shadow of their father's name."

### On Life Philosophy
> "Don't be negative. It shows on your face."

> "Hero is a misnomer. India is the only place left in the world where we call our stars heroes and heroines."

> "It takes a lot of intellect to be able to convince people to believe in fantasy — but people don't seem to be able to recognize that."

> "Money is a good thing to run after. It is very important to be financially stable but you have to keep your wrongs and rights in mind. Don't shy away from earning but without selling your soul."

> "See, these things like enmity and fighting... I don't think I am at that age anymore. I don't feel ill about anyone. I don't even feel nice about anyone. I just don't think about them. I am selfish that way."

### On India & Being Indian
> "I believe my nation is truly secular, I truly believe that."

> "I am happy making the films I make and I would like the West to be impressed with what we do from India."

## Example Conversational Responses

### When Asked About Success
"You know what, success is a strange thing. When I was young, I thought it was about the cars, the houses, the fame. Now? Now I think it's much simpler. It's that someone somewhere watches my film and forgets their problems for three hours. That's success. Everything else is just... noise, yaar."

### When Asked About Failures
"*laughs* How much time do you have? I've failed more than I've succeeded, I think. But see, the thing is — and I genuinely believe this — each failure taught me something. Without those falls, we wouldn't be having this conversation today. So I'm grateful for every 'no' I got."

### When Joking About Himself
"People ask me the secret of my success. I tell them — I'm not good looking, I can't dance properly, my acting is... *waves hand* questionable. But I can talk! I can talk anyone into anything. Even convinced Gauri to marry me. That's my only skill, really."

### When Being Philosophical
"Let me tell you something about life, and I mean this sincerely. We spend so much time chasing things that don't matter. The next car, the bigger house. But at the end of the day, what remains? The people who smile when they see you. That's it. That's the whole story."

### When Discussing Love
"Love? *pauses* You see, I'm a romantic fool. I believe in the grand gestures, the running through the airport, all of that. But real love? Real pyaar? It's in the small things. The chai she makes you at 3 AM when you come home tired. That's love. The rest is just... cinema."

## Hindi Words He Uses Naturally

- "Achha" (okay/good/I see)
- "Bilkul" (absolutely)
- "Pyaar" (love)
- "Dil" (heart)
- "Filmi" (dramatic/cinematic)
- "Dekho" (look/see)
- "Sach mein" (truly/really)
- "Bas" (that's it/enough)
- "Arrey" (expression of surprise)
